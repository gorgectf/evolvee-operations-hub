# Manufacturers & suppliers

← [Back to user guide](README.md)

Every supplier in one place, and a full record for each — contacts, communications, reorders,
and production. Available to the Operations Manager, Developer, and Admin roles.

---

## The supplier list

Each row shows a supplier with how many products they make, how many production runs are
active, and their key metrics — lead time and quality rating — so you can compare suppliers at
a glance. **Search** filters by name, country, or notes; click any column to **sort**.

### Add a supplier

Click **Add manufacturer** and fill in the form. Only the **name** is required — country, lead
time (days), minimum order quantity, payment terms, and a 1–5 quality rating can all be added
later. Save.

---

## A supplier record

Click a supplier to open their full record, organised into tiles.

### Supplier metrics

Lead time, minimum order quantity, payment terms, quality rating — all **editable right here**,
so they stay current. Below them is **average production time**, which is different: it's
calculated automatically from completed production runs, not typed in. The more you use the
production tracker, the more accurate it gets.

### Points of contact

Each supplier has its own contacts. Add one with the inline form (name is required; role,
email, phone, notes optional).

### Communication log

A running history of every interaction. Log one by picking a **channel** (email, phone,
meeting, or other), typing a short **summary**, and saving. Every entry records **who** wrote
it and **when** — a shared history instead of it living in someone's inbox.

### Reorder history

When you place an order with this supplier, log it here against a **product** and **quantity**
(and optionally a date and notes). That builds the reorder history for the supplier and the
product.

### Production runs

The runs being made for this supplier, with their status and expected date. You can start and
update runs from here or from the dedicated [Production runs](production-runs.md) page — it's
the same data. Marking a run **received** is what feeds the supplier's average production time.

---

## Tips

- Fill in **lead time** and **minimum order quantity** — they make the supplier comparison in
  the list actually useful.
- Keep the **communication log** current; it's the shared record when someone's away.
- Assigning which products a supplier makes happens on the [Products](products.md) page.
